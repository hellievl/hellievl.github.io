How to Make a Really Good Peanut Sauce

Not to toot my own horn or anything, but anytime I make something with peanut sauce in it somebody says something to the effect of "wow this peanut sauce is really good". I want the whole world to know I am not gatekeeping any kind of secret peanut sauce recipe, and I pretty much riff it every time, and you too can make it at home <3 Here's what I do.

The base of any delicious peanut sauce is, of course, peanut butter. A lot of people say you have to use this or that variety, or that the cheap stuff is best or something like that. The optimal peanut butter to use for your sauce is whatever is currently in your pantry. I have used many different kinds, ranging from jif to the fancy organic stuff that you grind yourself at the co op. The effect on consistency and quality is minimal. If your peanut butter has added salt or sugar, make a note of this and add less salt/sugar to the sauce than you might otherwise.

To the peanut butter base, we need to add a handful of key ingredients.

  1. SALT. Pretty straightforward. Ideally a mix of soy sauce and fish sauce, but one or the other will do just fine on its own. In a desperate situation you could probably just throw in a pinch of table salt (I have never done this, and I imagine you'd loose a lot of the depth of flavor that turns a good peanut sauce into a great peanut sauce).
  2. ACID. Wake it up..........my go to is lime juice, but I also like rice vinegar, apple cider vinegar, even lemon juice is fine. We want brightness! We want tang!
  3. SUGAR. Again self explanatory. I usually go for maple syrup or just a pinch of normal sugar.
  4. HOT WATER. Genuinely the most important ingredient (besides peanut butter itself), use hot water to control the sauce's consistency. If you're making a dipping sauce, or some kind of cold dressing, add water until it is exactly as thick as you like it. If you are adding it to noddles/something hot, make it significantly thinner. It's going to look really wet and watery, but trust me on this, it's going to thicken up a lot, like a lot a lot (I don't know why it does this). If the sauce is too think when it hits the heat it will become extremely gluey and unpleasant to eat. You will ruin your dinner.
  5. THE RIFFABLES. Here's where you get to have fun/clean out your pantry. Personally I love something to make it spicy. Lately I've been throwing in a tiny spoonful of this really hot chili oil Ola bought and it tastes awesome. Chili flakes or even a fresh thai chili chopped up really fine would also be welcome additions. Curry powder is another fun option. Gives it a really fun flavor. Sesame oil is good. Citrus zest offers a lot of bang for your buck. One time I swapped out the soy sauce for miso paste and that was really cool. I would caution against going too crazy, lest you lose sight of what actually makes this sauce good (salty sweet tangy peanut butter). Stick to the essentials and you will be just fine.
To actually make the sauce, all you need to do is add a conservative measure of the above ingredients into an adequately sized bowl and mix until homogenous (it looks kind of weird and gross for awhile, just keep mixing). Taste for balance. Too salty? Add a little more of everything else. Not tangy enough? Throw in some more lime juice. Too runny? Another spoonful of PB. Work slowly and add one thing at a time until it's tasting right. Trust your mouth !

It's really that easy. This sauce is great for dipping fresh spring rolls into, dressing a salad or roasted veggies, or whipping up some really excellent peanut noodles; one of my favorite easy lunches of all time is boiling some ramen, throwing the seasoning packet in the garbage, and tossing them with peanut sauce instead. Top with scallions and herbs and whole peanuts. Add tofu and veggies to make it serious.
